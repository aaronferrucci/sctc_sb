SCTC Super Bowl 1k/3k/10k

Aaron Ferrucci

February 8, 2016

On Saturday February 7, 2016, the Santa Cruz Track Club organized three footraces on the west side of Santa Cruz. The weather was perfect - cool and sunny, and it as a nice casual event. My son Ian and I participated in the 10k, which was a nice flat run from the back entrance of Natural Bridges State Park, down West Cliff drive past the lighthouse, almost to the Dream Inn, and then back along the same course.

The SCTC posted the race results online, so I decided to do a little data exploration and analysis.

First, some basic facts.

Where runners are from

3k results

42 runners participated in the 3k.

The gender distribution for the 3k is tilted somewhat toward female.

The average age of a 3k runner was 49.9.

The age histogram shows a pretty good age spread, from under 10 to over 80.

Plotting gender vs. age as a box plot shows that female runners are generally younger, and there’s a bit more variance in age among the male runners.

With a small number of runners, we don’t really have meaningful statistics, but nonetheless there seems to be a general upward of run time vs. age.

10k Results

108 runners participated in the 10k.

The gender balance for the 10k is tilted more toward male than the 3k was toward female.

The average age was 44.7.

The age histogram for the 10k race is something of a bell curve. There’s good representation in the 45-60 group.

As in the 3k, male 10k runners are older, on average, but it’s not as strong a difference.

Again, there’s a general upward trend of runtime vs. age, and males are typically faster.
